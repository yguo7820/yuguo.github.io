Plant and Microbial Ecology

Research Interest

My research focuses on the interactions between plants and belowground microorganisms (bacteria and fungi) and the covariation of both communities in response to environmental gradients. As primary producers and ecosystem engineers, land plants are the bedrock of terrestrial ecosystems. Plants support nearly the entire food web and are vital to multiple ecosystem functions, ultimately benefiting humans in profound ways. Soil and root-associated microbes also play important roles influencing soil processes and plant productivity. The importance of both groups transcends scales. Locally, beneficial and commensal microbes make nutrients bonded in soil and organic matter available for plants, whereas soil pathogens can cause plant diseases. Globally, decomposition of organic matter by microbes and the effects of microbes on plant productivity mediate atmospheric concentrations of greenhouse gasses, namely CO2 that fuel climate change.
